Welcome


The legacy of Manual High School and its Alumni has been established well over a century ago. Established September 7, 1909, Manual High School was opened on the corner of Lincoln and Shelley Streets as Manual Training High School. After 54 years, the Manual Training High School population increased to 1680 students. The school board decided to purchase land and build on the current Manual High School location on 811 South Griswold. On April 1, 2022, a group of concerned alumni formed the Manual High School Alumni Student Recognition Association (MASRA) to provide resources that promote excellence in academics, athletics, and the arts. The notion that excellent schools build excellent citizens and communities is central to the association’s philosophy. Your Alumni Association will be supporting our students while they are currently enrolled at Manual High School and its feeder middle and primary schools. MASRA will support the MHS athletes by building a new state of the art weight room. We will also help our teachers and administrators by providing resources to improve our students' math and reading placement scores. Also our fine arts’ numbers will increase for band, orchestra, and visual arts. MASRA will offer summer scholarships for band camps and other visual art camps for Manual High School Students. Our Association is here to support the legacy of Peoria Manual High School, its Alumni and Current Students. It’s through your generous donations that our mission can continue. We hope you consider pledging your continuing support so our operations may continue throughout the year. The General Fund is how we operate daily and your generous contributions make it happen. Please hit the “Donate to MASRA” button and support your Alumni Association! Consider an Endowment in your name, your family or a loved one. Our endowment program is another way you as an Alumnus can make a difference. Achievements

  • Created a Hall of Fame for Athletics and Distinguished Alumni
  • Naming of the street after former band instructor George G. Graves.
  • Naming the school fine arts wing the George G Graves Cultural Arts Center
  • Naming of street after former arts instructor Larry Hendricks
  • Hanging of Athletic Sponsorship Sign in Manual High School Gymnasium
  • The Black History Month program is the primary fundraiser for the Manual Drumline's trip to D.C.
  • Manual Faculty/Staff have created a school award as namesakes of each honoree. It will recognize the top-performing and visual arts student each school year (by faculty vote)
  • The Manual Scholarship Board is considering the creation of scholarships in the honorees' names. If approved, they will recognize performing and visual arts students.
